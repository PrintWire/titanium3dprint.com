## Ti6Al4V DMLS Parts

Ti6Al4V (Grade 5) is the workhorse titanium alloy for DMLS — the highest
strength-to-weight ratio material in the additive manufacturing catalog. 993–1,055
MPa UTS at 4.43 g/cm³. 30% lighter than stainless steel at comparable strength.
Certified to AMS 4999 (aerospace) and ASTM F136 (medical, using ELI grade).
Build envelopes to 400 × 400 × 450 mm. Stress relief and optional HIP available.


## Medical Titanium Implants

Ti6Al4V ELI (extra-low interstitial, Grade 23) for implantable devices. DMLS
enables patient-specific geometry from CT/MRI data, porous lattice surfaces
(40–80% porosity, 400–700 µm pore size) for bone ingrowth, and complex internal
channels for vascular access devices. ISO 13485 quality management. FDA 510(k)
technical file documentation available.


## Aerospace Titanium

Aerospace titanium printing delivers mass reduction while matching wrought mechanical
performance. Topology optimization routinely achieves 30–60% mass reduction versus
conventional machined equivalents. Hydraulic manifolds with internal channel networks,
bracket clusters consolidated from 5+ machined parts to 1 DMLS part, and ducting
with variable cross-section for thermal management. AMS 4999, AS9100D, NADCAP
heat treatment certification available.


## Post-Processing

Titanium DMLS post-processing: stress relief (800°C / 2h vacuum) mandatory before
support removal → HIP (900°C / 100 MPa / 2h) for fatigue-critical applications →
CNC machining on bearing surfaces and thread features → surface finishing.
Type II anodizing for color-coding and mild corrosion protection. Type III hard
anodize for wear surfaces. Electropolish for medical and food-contact applications.
